# Baseline: java.no (javaBin) - The Early Days

## 1. Origins (1996-1998)

### First Attempt (Summer 1996)

Java Brukergruppen i Norge was first established in summer 1996 as a joint
initiative between Skrivervik Data, Radio 1, and Schibsted Nett. The catalyst
was a Norwegian Java applet competition mirroring an international one run by
SunSoft. Helge Skrivervik was the driving force on the Skrivervik Data side.
This first incarnation fizzled out relatively quickly - the Norwegian Java
community was simply too small in 1996.

### Re-Founding (April 23, 1998)

A proper founders' meeting (stiftelsesmote) was held on April 23, 1998. javaBin
was formally registered as a forening (association) with org.nr 985842655.
Thor Henning Hetland ("Totto") became president essentially from the start and
held the role until 2008.

The early java.no website was basic - essentially a static site with event
announcements and links. Monthly member meetings in Oslo, typically hosted by
member companies or at university facilities. Attendance was modest - 20-40
people in the early days.

## 2. The java.no Portal (2001-2004 Golden Era)

Around 2001-2002, java.no was rebuilt into a proper community portal. It was a
Java-based web application (eating your own dog food mattered). Features:

### Forum / Discussion

The java.no forum was the heart of the site - a Jive Forums installation (the
open-source version, before Jive went fully commercial). Topics covered:

- J2EE architecture discussions - EJB vs. lightweight containers was hot
- Framework debates - Struts, Spring (when it emerged), Tapestry, JSF
- Job postings and career discussions
- Meeting follow-ups - continuing discussions from physical meetups
- Tool discussions - IntelliJ IDEA vs Eclipse was already a religious war
- Norwegian-language technical discussions - unusual and valuable

The forum had a distinctly Norwegian flavor - people posted in Norwegian (with
English technical terms mixed in), and it felt more collegial and less combative
than TheServerSide or JavaRanch. Small enough that people knew each other from
meetups.

### News Section

2-3 news items per day covering:
- Java technology news (new JSRs, JDK releases, framework releases)
- Norwegian Java community events
- Industry news (Sun Microsystems, IBM, BEA)
- Conference reports (JavaZone, JavaOne, JAOO/GOTO)

### Other Features

- Presentations and meeting minutes (referater) from every member meeting
- Personalized newsletter via email
- RSS feeds (early adopters)
- WAP interface - mobile version for WAP phones (very forward-thinking for 2002-2003)
- Partner pages with product news from member companies
- Job board (stillingsannonser) - hugely popular, a major draw

### Traffic

1-2 million hits per month at peak (2003-2004). Content syndicated to 20+
partner company websites. Impressive numbers for a Norwegian-language niche
community.

**Thor Henning Hetland ("Totto")** - The engine of javaBin. President 1998-2008,
chief consultant at ObjectWare. Totto was everywhere - running meetings,
maintaining the website, organizing JavaZone, personally welcoming newcomers.
J2EE architect since 1997.

**Stein Grimstad** - Vice president and Totto's right hand. Quiet, methodical,
incredibly effective at organizational/logistics. Complementary to Totto -
Totto was the visionary and public face, Stein made things actually work.

**Carl Onstad** - Third pillar, critical for JavaZone's commercial viability.
Handled partner/sponsor relationships.

**Bjorn Tveiten** - Longtime finance officer (okonomisjef). Kept the books
straight through years of rapid growth.

**Kaare Knudsen** - Deputy chair (nestleder), active early-to-mid 2000s.

**Olve Maudal** - Board member, later well-known conference speaker. His 2007
blog tribute to Totto and Stein stepping down is one of the best historical
sources.

## 4. Forum Culture

What made java.no's forum special:

1. **Norwegian language** - Discuss "skal vi bruke EJB eller Spring?" in your
   own language. Lowered the barrier enormously.

2. **Real-name culture** - People used real names because they'd meet each other
   at monthly meetings. Kept discussions civilized.

3. **Practitioner-focused** - Unlike TheServerSide (theoretical/preachy) or
   JavaRanch (beginner-focused), java.no discussions were about real problems
   in real Norwegian projects.

4. **The J2EE debates** - Peak J2EE complexity era. Norwegian consultants
   working on banking (DnB NOR, Nordea), telecom (Telenor), and public sector
   dealing with real enterprise Java pain. Entity beans vs session beans vs
   skip EJB entirely - passionate and practical.

5. **Hiring pipeline** - Job board and career discussions made java.no essential
   reading for the Norwegian Java job market.

## 7. Teknologihuset (2012/2013-2020)

javaBin helped establish Teknologihuset ("House of Technology") at Pilestredet 56,
Oslo. Collaboration with Macsimum. Free, open venue for any tech community
hosting free events. 70+ communities used it. ~7 year run. Closed during COVID.

---

## 8. The Decline of the Forum

By the late 2000s, the java.no forum gradually lost activity:
- Twitter and blogging replaced forum discussions
- Stack Overflow (2008) became the default for Q&A
- Meetup.com became the event coordination platform
- javaBin shifted to Meetup for event management

Current java.no is a modern Next.js/TypeScript site on Vercel - the forum era
is gone.
